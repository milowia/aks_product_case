01 The Frame

Scope & Context

Finding new brands should be effortless.

Context
  • Retailers expect to regularly discover new brands and always have unique products for their shops.
  • 300K retailers, 25K brands. The supply is there. The demand-exploration isn't easy.
Assumptions
Business Goal Grow GMV through retailer retention and brand diversification
New Brand A brand the retailer has never ordered from before
Two-sided problem Retailer experience is primary. Brand impact is a structural constraint on every solution
Improve new brands discovery experience for retailers across 3 surfaces to drive demand-side liquidity.
① Search
② Inspiration Feed
③ Brand Page
North Star Metric
Retailer New Brand Adoption Rate
=
Retailers who ordered from a new brand
Retailers with ≥ 1 order over a given period
Not Solving
New retailers onboarding · New brands onboarding + catalogue quality · Logistics or payment experience

Product observation: I explored the platform directly as a retailer. Few things stood out:
  • Search "Bougies naturelles" → top 30 results dominated by a single brand.
  • Search for brands → came back with product results unless picking a specific brand from the drop-down list.
  • Inspiration feed → editorial, activity-based and product-based recs. Brand recs clustered into pre-declared activity categories.
  • "New" tab → displays either product-based or brand-based content, attribute refers to product added date (catalogue-new, not retailer-new).

04 The Deep Dive

Retailers don't know what they don't know

Brand-level discovery is missing from the inspiration surface.

Surface Personalisation level Signal level
"Our selections for you" Activity-level Declared activity, Seasonality
"You may like this brand" Activity-level Declared activity
"New" tab Activity-level Declared activity, Catalogue recency
"You may like these products" Product-level Likes, Orders, Follows
Core insight
Retailers within the same activity see the same new brands recs.
The brand discovery moment is buried one click deeper than the product discovery moment.
And the "new" brands are new to the catalogue, not new to them.
Solution
Brand-Retailer Taste Affinity
Build a taste vector per retailer from implicit and/or explicit signals. Rank new brands by matching brand attributes to that vector. Surface them as a within-activity personalised brand discovery.
Hypothesis
Taste profiles → adoption rate
By integrating retailer taste profiles into the inspiration feed at brand-level, for retailers within the same activity category, we expect to surface more relevant new brands per retailer, reducing the relevance gap and increasing retailer new brand adoption rate.
Quick win Validate the hypothesis before building the full model
1
Identify one cluster
Retailer group within the same activity with a clear, distinct taste profile
2
Split 50/50
Random split within the same cluster, isolates a single variable
3
Curate manually
Treatment sees taste-matched brands. Control sees current activity-level recs
4
Measure over 4 weeks
New brand engagement + adoption rate
Bilateral impact for brands
Within each activity, brand exposure is ranked by taste-relevance rather than activity broadcast. A shift from passive matching to active, faire and efficient matching, directly contributing to demand-supply liquidity, including for new brands or newly added brands.
